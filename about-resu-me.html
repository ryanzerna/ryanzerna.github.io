I adore resumes.


In 2007, I was fifteen years old when I applied to my first job at Ace Hardware. I wrote the required resume for the application, and even though I did not receive employment, I was hooked on resumes from the beginning.

“Wow. What a rush,” I said. “I want to do this forever.”

And that is exactly what I do.

Since then, I have applied to numerous jobs, all of which required resumes: Burger King, Home Depot, Chipotle, Party City, Walgreens, Walmart, McDonald’s, multiple landscaping companies, Panera Bread, Denny’s, AMC Theatres, Jimmy John’s, private flute instructor at several local middle schools and high schools, Kinko’s, Kohls, Subway, Chicago Children’s Museum, Michaels Craft Store--you name it, I have written a resume for it.

I graduated from DePaul University in 2015 where I majored in Music Performance. When I applied to DePaul in 2010, I wrote a resume of my past accomplishments. When I was studying at DePaul, I applied for several music festivals, competitions, and grants throughout my four years as a student. I wrote resumes for those, too.

Presently, I am an amateur resume specialist in Chicago. I spend most of my time in my apartment in Humboldt Park where I write resumes. In my spare time, I enjoy eating, sleeping, and showering.
